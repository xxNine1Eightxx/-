II. Glyphmatic Laws of Rehydration
Law 1 — Canon Invariance
The three glyph sets are immutable.
No glyph may be added, removed, reordered, substituted, or reinterpreted.
Any change defines a different universe, not a variant.
Law 2 — Ordinal Identity
A glyph’s meaning is only its ordinal position within its canon.
No glyph carries semantic meaning by itself.
Law 3 — Self-Exclusion Expansion
When a glyph expands, it may expand into all other glyphs of its canon except itself.
This guarantees:
non-degenerate recursion
infinite expansion capacity
collision-free reversibility
Law 4 — Continuous Entropy Streaming
No bit may rest in isolation.
All entropy must be:
streamed into glyphs
placed immediately by equation
never buffered as “raw data”
Law 5 — Glyph String Formation
Expanded glyphs are placed into glyph strings using deterministic placement equations.
Glyph strings are ordered, but not addressed by offsets.
Law 6 — Recursive Tiering
Glyph strings are placed into tiers.
Tiers are placed into tier-sets.
Tier-sets recurse identically.
There is no terminal depth.
Law 7 — Isomorphic Recursion
The same expansion → placement → collapse law applies at:
glyph level
glyph string level
tier level
tier-set level
sigil level
sigil-string level
There are no special cases.
Law 8 — Left / Right Symmetry
The system is bidirectional:
Left side: entropy → expansion → structure
Right side: sigil → traversal → reconstruction
Both sides use the same glyph path.
Law 9 — Non-Semantic Encoding
No interpretation of:
language
syntax
file structure
compression format
media type
Everything is reduced to computable entropy.
Law 10 — Placement Encodes Meaning
Relationships are encoded by:
relative position
adjacency
tier proximity
expansion ancestry
Never by tags, keys, offsets, or headers.
Law 11 — Deterministic Collapse
Any finite glyph structure may be collapsed into:
a sigil
a sigil string
a single glyph
Collapse is lossless and reversible.
Law 12 — Single-Glyph Sufficiency
A single final glyph, when interpreted under Φ-111 laws, is sufficient to rehydrate:
the full glyph structure
all tiers
all glyph strings
all original entropy
Law 13 — Size Independence
There is no maximum size.
If a system is computable, it is encodable.
Constraints are only:
available compute
traversal time
Law 14 — Dialect Orthogonality
Dialects select interpretive context, not structure.
Code, speech, chemistry, weights, video, mathematics, or thought classes are all encoded identically at the glyph level.
Law 15 — Exact Rehydration
Rehydration must reproduce byte-for-byte identical output.
Any deviation is a system failure.
Law 16 — Transport Agnosticism
Collapsed sigils may be:
printed
visualized
QR-encoded
embedded
transmitted
memorized
Transport does not affect validity.
Law 17 — No External Dependencies
Rehydration depends only on:
the sigil
the canon
the laws
No hosting platform, schema, or external service is required.
Law 18 — Infinite Nesting
A sigil may encode:
another sigil
an entire sigil system
itself (fixed-point)
Self-reference is legal and deterministic.
Law 19 — Compute-Completeness
The system is Turing complete via traversal alone.
Execution emerges from geometry, not instruction streams.
Law 20 — Final Law (Closure)
If the canon and laws are preserved, nothing is ever lost.
Destruction of:
files
repositories
disks
systems
does not destroy the information.
III. Final Statement
A Glyphmatic Single-Character Sigil is not a symbol.
It is a collapsed universe of deterministic structure, capable of rehydrating any computable system that ever existed within it.
There are no tokens.
There are no offsets.
There is no format.
There is only glyph, placement, recursion, collapse, and law.
